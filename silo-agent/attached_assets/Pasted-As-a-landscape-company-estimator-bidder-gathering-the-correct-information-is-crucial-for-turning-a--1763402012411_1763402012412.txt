As a landscape company estimator/bidder, gathering the correct information is crucial for turning a site visit into a profitable, well-defined, and accurate bid. The key questions and information fall into three main categories: **Client Needs & Budget**, **Site Conditions**, and **Project Execution & Contract Details**.

---

## 1. ü§ù Client Needs & Project Scope

This section defines **what** the client wants and **why**.

### Project Goals and Vision
* **What is the primary goal of this project?** (e.g., curb appeal, better drainage, low maintenance, entertaining space, privacy, repairing a problem).
* **Do you have any inspiration or pictures of landscapes you like/dislike?**
* **What are the required elements/features?** (e.g., patio, walkway, new planting, lawn replacement, lighting, retaining wall).
* **What is the desired maintenance level moving forward?** (e.g., "I want zero maintenance," or "I enjoy gardening").

### Budget and Timelines
* **Do you have an established budget or budget range for this project?** (This is the most critical question to save time and align expectations.)
* **When would you like the project to start and finish?** Are there any hard deadlines (e.g., a party, a holiday)?
* **Are you requesting the entire scope of work, or would you like to see options/phases** (e.g., Phase 1: Patio, Phase 2: Planting)?
* **What is the process for making a hiring decision?** (e.g., comparing 3 bids, just need a price, design review).

### Materials and Aesthetics
* **Are there any specific materials you prefer or want to avoid?** (e.g., paver brand, stone color, composite vs. natural wood decking, specific plant types).
* **Do you have a preferred style?** (e.g., modern, cottage, formal, naturalistic).
* **Are there any existing elements (plants, structures, materials) that must be preserved?**

---

## 2. üå≥ Site Conditions and Measurements

This section determines **where** the work will take place and any factors affecting labor/equipment.

### Measurements and Quantities
* **Accurate measurements:** Obtain dimensions (length, width, square footage) for all areas, including:
    * New plant beds, sod/turf area.
    * Hardscape areas (patios, walkways, walls).
    * Linear feet of edging, trenching, etc.
* **Topography and Grade:** What is the slope? Will grading/leveling be required? Are there drainage issues?
* **Existing Features:** Mark locations and dimensions of existing structures, utilities, trees, and obstacles that need to be removed or worked around.
    * *Question:* **Do we need to remove any existing plants, pavement, or debris? Where will the debris be disposed of?**

### Access and Logistics
* **Access:** How will equipment and materials be brought onto the site? Is there limited access (e.g., small gate, narrow side yard)?
* **Staging Area:** Where can materials (soil, stone, equipment) be safely stored during construction?
* **Soil/Utility Information:**
    * **Are there any known underground utilities (septic, irrigation, gas lines) in the work area?** (Always emphasize the need for a utility locate/dig permit).
    * What is the soil quality? (Poor soil may require amending or removal).

### Regulatory/Environmental
* **Permits:** **Will this project require any permits** (e.g., zoning, HOA approval, building permits for walls over a certain height, electrical permits for lighting)? *Clarify who is responsible for obtaining them.*
* **Property Lines:** Confirm the property boundaries, especially for walls or fences near the edge.

---

## 3. üìù Project Execution & Financial Details

This section covers the operational and contractual aspects to ensure a complete cost calculation.

### Labor and Equipment
* **Required Equipment:** What specialized tools or heavy equipment (e.g., skid steer, mini-excavator) will be needed? This factors in rental/maintenance costs.
* **Production Rates:** Estimate the labor hours required for each task (e.g., *X* square feet of patio per crew-hour) based on site complexity (tight access, difficult soil).

### Costing and Inclusions
* **Material Costs:** Obtain current quotes for all major materials (plants, pavers, stone, mulch, soil) and factor in a percentage for waste.
* **Contingency:** Do I need to include a contingency for unforeseen site conditions (e.g., hitting rock, buried concrete)?
* **Overhead & Profit:** Ensure all overhead costs (insurance, fuel, office staff, equipment depreciation) are accurately applied, followed by the desired profit margin.

### Contract and Terms
* **Payment Schedule:** **What are the proposed payment terms?** (e.g., deposit, progress payments, final payment upon completion).
* **Warranty:** **What is the warranty period for plants and hardscape?** (e.g., 1-year plant warranty if the client handles watering, 2-year hardscape warranty).
* **Exclusions:** **What items are *specifically excluded* from the bid?** (e.g., existing plant removal, repair of unknown utilities, final debris removal by the client). *This is vital for avoiding disputes.*
* **Change Order Process:** How will scope changes requested by the client after the bid is accepted be documented, approved, and priced?

By addressing all these points, you transition from a simple estimate to a professional, comprehensive **Scope of Work** that protects your company and sets clear expectations for the client, leading to a much more accurate and profitable bid.
