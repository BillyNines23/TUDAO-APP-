3. Lawn Care and Landscaping Services:
3.1. During the growing season, Contractor shall mow, edge and trim lawns to maintain an even, wellgroomed appearance. Sidewalks, curb runs and planting areas shall be edged and weeded. Trees, shrubs,
groundcover, flower beds, and ornamental grasses shall be properly maintained and groomed in a healthy, vigorous
growing condition, free from disease and concentrations of pests. Any dead tree or shrub shall be brought to the
Store/Club Managers attention. All trash and debris shall be removed from sidewalks, gutters, and all planted areas.
In no case shall trash, litter, or leaves be blown or swept by Contractor onto the property of others. All trash, litter,
leaves, branches, cuttings etc. shall be collected, hauled away, and disposed of by Contractor away from the
premises at a certified green waste facility or in a lawful manner. Contractor shall fertilize all grass areas_____
times per year. All chemical applications shall be applied at the rate specified by the manufacturer and during the
appropriate season, as needed. Weeds in planted areas, sidewalks, curbs, gutters, or pavement shall be removed or
killed weekly as the weeds emerge. Contractor must ensure that the application of chemicals is done by a licensed
applicator, if required by any state, local, or federal regulations.
3.2 Contractor shall be solely responsible (at its sole expense) for verifying and obtaining any
necessary tree trimming permits as required with any applicable authority with jurisdiction. Contractor will secure
all permits prior to performing the service. Contractor shall annually prune trees that are less than fourteen (14)
feet tall to remove dead, diseased, broken, dangerous, or crossing branches. Contractor must receive written
Walmart approval prior to pruning trees that are fourteen (14) feet or taller. Any tree or shrub found dead due to
negligent acts by the Contractor should be replaced within two (2) weeks of death at the Contractor’s sole expense.
3.3 A Pre-Service walk through shall identify routine fertilizer, herbicide, trees (under 14’) and shrub
trimming and mulch application frequencies. These services shall manage the existing vegetation in a health
manicured condition while being environementally friendly. Mulch shall be maintained to a 4” minimum
throughout the season, contractor to top-dress with matching color and material. Excessive neglect to mulching or
other items deamed necessary to maintain a well groomed appearance need to be approved by Walmart.
3.4 If applicable, Contractor shall ensure the premises irrigation system is in good and working
condition and adjusted to provide sufficient irrigation to lawn, shrub, and tree areas. This includes regular
monitoring the irrigation sprinkler system to insure that watering times are properly set to maintain first class
conditions. Contractor shall conserve water and assure that all-watering rules and regulations are followed. Any
penalties, fines, or citations related to watering ordinance violations shall be paid by Contractor. Contractor is
responsible for properly winterizing and spring start up of the irrigation system. Contractor shall make Minor
Repairs to the irrigation system as defined herein. Walmart will make no additional payment to Contractor for said
Minor Repairs. Major Repairs to the irrigation system such as pump failures, etc. shall be reported to Walmart and
repaired according to a separate written agreement between Walmart and Contractor. “Minor Repairs” include, but
are not limited to, clearing out irrigation lines, adjustments of spray heads and nozzles, adjusting zone controls,
programming main controller, maintenance of spray heads and nozzles, including replacement or minor parts,
minor leaks in lines, risers, laterals or other system components.
3.5 Remotely operated irrigation clocks shall have a coordinated effort with the third party monitoring
vendor to insure all changes to the clock are coordinated by end of business.

4. Miscellaneous:
4.1. Contractor shall provide all at Contractor's own risk and sole expense, all labor, materials, tools,
equipment, insurance, transportation, hauling, dumping, and all other items needed to provide the Services outlined
in this Scope of Work.
4.2. Contractor shall make minor replacements and repairs to the landscape facilities as part of the
required Services set forth herein. Major items needing replacement or repair shall be reported to Walmart's
authorized representative within 48 hours of occurrence. Any facilities or property damaged or destroyed as a result
of Contractor's operations on the premises shall be repaired or replaced at Contractor's sole expense.
4.3. All chemicals shall be used in accordance with label directions, the manufacturers’ recommended
handling methods, and all applicable regulations. Registered chemicals shall be used only on the advice of a
qualified, licensed, if required, pest control advisor. Nothing in this specification shall be construed to be the advice
of, or to substitute for the advice of, a pest control adviser.
4.4. Pesticides shall not be applied within one hour of the start of operating hours of the facility. In the
event that it is not possible to complete the application by one hour prior to business hours (i.e., 24-hour locations),
applications shall be made at times when customer presence is minimal. Areas to be treated shall be blocked off and
warning signs posted. Contractor shall take precautions to keep persons away from pesticide and herbicide-treated
areas until the applied material is fully dry and the treated area is safe for entry. Contractor shall follow the
recommendations of the pesticide manufacturer and all applicable governmental and industry regulations.
4.5 Contractor shall NOT perform any maintenance activities including mowing, herbicide, pesticide
or fertilizer applications within any stormwater conveyance or collection area, wetland or natural preservation areas
including wet retention ponds, dry detention ponds, ditches, swales, bio-retention areas or any other component of
the stormwater system.
4.6 Contractor agrees to warrant any new items installed against any defects in materials or
workmanship. Contractor hereby warrants that it will repair or replace any new materials installed by Contractor
found to be defective for a one (1) year period following the date of the signed work order completion report.
Contractor shall mow only to the crest (top) of the slope and not mow (or perform other maintenance activities,
with the exception of trash pickup) the side slopes (bank) or bottom. Contractor shall not apply any chemicals
directly or indirectly into any stormwater conveyance or collection areas. Except for within open water areas,
Contractor shall remove all debris and trash from all areas of retention or detention ponds or wetland areas. No
motorized equipment is to be operated or utilized inside the perimeter of any stormwater conveyance or collection
area.
4.7 If any facility subject to this Scope of Work is located within the State of California and if the
Services performed pursuant to this Scope of Work for such facility fall within the scope of California Labor Code
Section 2810, Contractor shall complete Attachment 1 to the Agreement in its entirety and both Walmart and
Contractor shall execute Attachment 1. The execution of Attachment 1 is required in addition to, and not in place
of, the execution of the Agreement and this Scope of Work by Contractor and Wal-Mart. Contractor shall comply
in all respects with all of the terms, conditions and obligations in Attachment 1. The terms and provisions of
Attachment 1 are hereby incorporated by reference into, and made a part of, this Scope of Work.
